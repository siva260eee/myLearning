### Case 1: Premium Smartphone - High Credit Score Customer

**Scenario**: iPhone 15 Pro Max financing for customer with excellent credit

**Customer Profile**:
- Credit Score: 780 (Excellent)
- Monthly Income: $6,500
- Existing Loans: 0
- Preferred Term: 24 months

**Device**: iPhone 15 Pro Max - $1,199

**Financing Options**:
1. **0% APR 12-month** - $99.92/month, $1,199 total (Best for fast payoff)
2. **0% APR 24-month** - $49.96/month, $1,199 total (Best overall - matches preference)
3. **5% APR 36-month** - $29.89/month, $1,276 total (Lower payment but costs more)

**Agent Decision**: Option 2 (24-month 0% APR)
- Matches customer preference
- No interest charges
- Affordable monthly payment
- No early payoff penalty

**Learning Points**:
- Excellent credit unlocks best rates
- Customer preference matters when cost is equal
- 0% APR options save significant money

## ðŸ§  Key Insights for AI Agents

1. **Credit Score Ranges**:
   - 740+: Unlock 0% APR options
   - 670-739: Access to most financing with reasonable rates
   - 580-669: Limited options, higher rates
   - <580: Very limited, may need secured or cosigner

2. **Affordability Rules**:
   - Ideal: Monthly payment < 10% of income
   - Acceptable: 10-15% of income
   - Risky: > 15% of income

3. **Special Circumstances**:
   - **Students**: Cosigners unlock better terms
   - **Seniors**: Simplicity > optimization, consider discounts
   - **Business**: Tax implications, depreciation schedules
   - **Loyal Customers**: Relationship value matters
   - **Variable Income**: Extra affordability buffer needed

4. **Decision Priorities by Customer Type**:
   - **High Credit**: Maximize 0% APR benefits
   - **Average Credit**: Balance affordability and cost
   - **Low Credit**: Focus on affordability and credit building
   - **Corporate**: Consider tax, depreciation, cash flow
   - **Students**: Leverage cosigners, student programs
   - **Seniors**: Prefer simplicity and discounts
